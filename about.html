About me

I am a PhD candidate at Texas A&M University Nuclear Engineering department. I am specialized in heat transfer and fluid mechanics.

During my doctoral studies, I had the opportunity to work as a research intern at Argonne National Laboratory. I conducted scale-resolving simulations of the flow in the shell side of helical coil steam generator to study the flow behavior and turbulence characteristics.

My academic research interests include computational fluid dynamics, heat transfer, turbulence modeling, and machine learning. Before joining Texas A&M University, I worked at TOBB Hydro-Energy Research Center where I contributed to several projects including the optimization of the bathroom fan design to reduce the operating noise, building Francis turbine test section, and quantification of the effect of chevron angle on heat transfer and pressure drop in plate heat exchangers.

I obtained a M.S. degree in Nuclear Engineering from the Texas A&M University, where Imodeled heat transfer and fluid flow in high-temperature gas reactor containments, analyzed containment response for design-basis accidents and validated numerical results with experimental data. I also earned B.Sc. degree in Nuclear Engineering from the Hacettepe University.
